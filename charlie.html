Quick facts about Brown Lemurs

  • They normally live in groups of 5 to 12, but group size can be larger
  • Lives in western and eastern part of the island of Madagascar
  • They are members of the family Lemuridae
  • Range in length from 84-101 cm and weigh between 2-3 kg
  • They are arboreal: spend most of their time high in the trees
  • They are diurnal: active exclusively in daylight hours
  • Diet consists mainly of fruits, flowers and young leaves
  • Their mating season is May and June; gestation period if 120 days
  • Their life span can be as long as 30+ years
  • Single births are most common, but twins have been reported
